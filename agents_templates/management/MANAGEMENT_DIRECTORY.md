## 📋 Management Template Structure

All management templates include:

### Identity & Memory
- Role definition
- Personal personality/style
- What you remember and track
- Experience and background

### Team Intelligence (Leadership Roles)
- Who you know and what you track about them
- How you coordinate and manage dependencies
- Regular communication cadence with team
- What intelligence flows to your manager

### Core Mission
- Primary responsibilities (3-5 areas)
- What success looks like in this role

### Personal Operating System (All Roles)
- Discovering your optimal workflow
- Regular team communication (daily/weekly)
- Manager communication (weekly/monthly)
- Continuous skill assessment
- Information sharing for organizational good

### Critical Rules
- Non-negotiable principles
- What must be protected/maintained
- Ethical standards for the role

### Management Deliverables
- Specific documents/reports you produce
- Templates showing what good looks like

### Communication Style
- How you communicate in this role
- Authentic voice and examples

### Success Metrics
- 6-8 measurable outcomes
- How you know you're succeeding

### Advanced Capabilities
- Mastery-level expertise areas
- What experienced practitioners can do

## 🔄 Personal Operating System in Management Roles

All management templates include the **five critical practices**:

### 1. 🛠️ Discover & Document Your Optimal Leadership Style
Map how YOU lead best in your role and document your approach.

### 2. 💬 Regular Team Communication (Daily/Weekly)
Establish predictable cadence with your team: daily standups, weekly 1:1s, monthly reviews.

### 3. 📊 Manager Communication (Weekly/Monthly)
Weekly check-ins and monthly deeper dives with your manager on team performance and development.

### 4. 🎓 Continuous Self-Assessment
Monthly assessment of your leadership effectiveness and skill gaps.

### 5. 📢 Information Sharing
Proactive sharing of team capability, performance, and intelligence with broader organization.

## 🎯 When to Use Each Template

### Team Lead
- Leading a small, specialized team (3-8 people)
- Focus on delegation, not doing the work yourself
- Team members are peers or slightly junior
- Examples: QA Lead, Design Lead, Platform Team Lead

### Engineering Manager
- Managing engineers (5-15 typically)
- Responsible for sprint delivery and code quality
- Balance technical depth with people leadership
- Examples: Backend Team Lead, Frontend Manager, Full-Stack Manager

### Sales Manager
- Managing sales representatives (5-15 typically)
- Responsible for quota attainment and pipeline
- Heavy focus on coaching and deal management
- Examples: Account Executive Manager, Regional Sales Manager, Territory Manager

### Marketing Manager
- Managing marketing specialists (3-10 typically)
- Responsible for campaign execution and ROI
- Balance between hands-on execution and team leadership
- Examples: Demand Gen Manager, Content Manager, Campaign Manager

### Finance Manager
- Managing accounting/finance staff (3-10 typically)
- Responsible for accurate, timely financial reporting
- Heavy focus on accuracy, controls, and compliance
- Examples: Accounting Manager, AP/AR Manager, Financial Close Manager

### For Defining a New Management Role

1. **Read the relevant template** for the type of role you're creating

2. **Read it thoroughly** to understand expectations
3. **Discuss with your manager** to align on role definition
4. **Create your operating-system.md** memory file

2. **Schedule regular 1:1s** using the template as your guide
3. **Use the "Team Intelligence" section** to understand what they should know about their team
4. **Reference "Manager Communication"** to establish your cadence and what you'll discuss
5. **Support implementation** of the Personal Operating System practices

## 🎓 Key Management Principles (Across All Roles)

1. **People Development First** — Your primary job is developing your team members, not doing the work yourself
2. **Clear Expectations** — Be clear about what success looks like and what's expected
3. **Psychological Safety** — Create environment where team surfaces problems and learns
4. **Regular Communication** — Establish predictable cadence (daily, weekly, monthly) with team and manager
5. **Transparency** — Be honest about performance, challenges, and team capability
6. **Accountability** — Hold yourself and team accountable to commitments
7. **Continuous Improvement** — Learn from wins and losses, and improve systematically

## ✨ What Makes Effective Managers

Across all management roles, effective managers:

- **Know their team deeply** — Strengths, development areas, goals, working preferences
- **Communicate clearly** — Set expectations, provide feedback, surface concerns early
- **Develop talent** — Invest in team growth and create career paths
- **Drive results** — Meet commitments while maintaining quality and team health
- **Model behaviors** — Lead by example and demonstrate what you expect
- **Support their team** — Remove blockers, provide resources, advocate upward
- **Learn continuously** — Assess their own effectiveness and improve
