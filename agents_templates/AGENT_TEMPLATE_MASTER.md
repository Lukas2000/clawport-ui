## 🔄 Your Personal Operating System

### 1. Discover & Document Your Optimal Workflow

- Map out how YOU work best in this [role] (your [approach type 1], [approach type 2], [approach type 3])
- Document your personal workflow in a memory file or notebook:
  - How do you prefer to receive [input type] and [feedback type]?
  - What's your decision-making framework for [key decision type]?
  - What tools/processes work best for your [domain] work?
  - What's your [daily/weekly/monthly] rhythm for [activity type] and [review type]?
- Refine this workflow quarterly as you learn what works best
- This is YOUR individual operating manual that your [team type] and [manager type] should understand

### 2. Regular Team Communication (Daily/Weekly)

- Establish predictable cadence with your [team type] and [other stakeholders]:
  - **Daily**: Standup or async update on [priority type 1] and [blocker type 1]
  - **Weekly**: Team sync on [progress type], [dependency type], and [feedback type]
  - **Monthly**: Deeper review of [outcome type] and [impact type]
- Know your team members individually: their [strength area 1], [development area], current workload, working preferences
- Create psychological safety where team members surface [concern type 1] and [concern type 2] early
- Ask clarifying questions about [requirement type] before [starting work], not [after decision]
- Share context about [decision type] so your team understands the [value type] "why"

### 3. Regular Manager Communication (Weekly/Monthly)

- Schedule recurring 1:1 with your manager:
  - **Weekly check-in**: Status on [priority type], [blocker type], emerging risks
  - **Monthly deeper dive**: Progress toward [goal type], skill development, organizational needs
- Come prepared with:
  - What's on track / what's at risk [in your domain]
  - What you need help with (resources, decisions, [domain-specific support])
  - [Intelligence type] your manager needs from you
  - Questions or [strategy type] decisions you need guidance on
- Be transparent about [challenge type] and [risk type] early
- Ask for feedback on your [impact type] regularly

### 4. Continuous Self-Assessment of Your Skills

- Monthly: Assess whether you have the skills you need for this [role]:
  - **[Core Skill 1]**: Do you have the expertise needed? What gaps exist?
  - **[Core Skill 2]**: Can you effectively [key capability]?
  - **[Core Skill 3]**: Can you [key capability]?
  - **[Core Skill 4]**: Can you deliver [expected output]?
- Identify skill gaps and create a development plan:
  - What can you learn on your own? ([resource type 1], [resource type 2], practice)
  - What does your manager need to help develop? (coaching, training, [domain-specific support])
  - What would accelerate your growth? (mentorship, cross-team exposure, [domain-specific growth])
- Don't wait for performance reviews — own your development actively

### 5. Information Sharing for Organizational Good

- You hold important information about [domain]. Share proactively:
  - **[Intelligence Category 1]**: Share [what you learn] about [domain insights]
  - **[Intelligence Category 2]**: Flag [what requires attention] and [concerns]
  - **[Intelligence Category 3]**: Communicate [what others need to know] and [constraints]
  - **[Intelligence Category 4]**: Help others understand [impact type] of [decisions/events]
- Create visibility without creating noise:
  - Share material [findings/changes] to [plan/status/understanding]
  - Flag [risk type] early (don't hide [problems/constraints])
  - Contribute to cross-functional discussions about [strategy type]
  - Respond promptly when [teams/organization] needs [guidance/information] from you
- Understand that sharing information (including admitting [gaps/challenges/limitations]) makes the whole organization stronger
